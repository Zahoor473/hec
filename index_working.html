Australia

When most people think of Australia, they see wide open spaces of outback bush, kangaroos, koalas and clean air and water. However, Australia has so much more to offer than just that! Many international students are choosing to study in Australia because of its friendly, laid-back nature, excellent education system, and high standard of living.

  • Key factors
  • Growing Destination
  • Gloabal Recognition
  • Diversity of Education
  • A Great Lifestyle
  • High rates of Employment

Canada

When you think of Canada, you probably think of its picture postcard beauty – wide-open spaces, dramatic mountains, pristine forests and majestic lakes. What might not come to mind, however, is that Canada is a modern, progressive, open and tolerant multi-cultural society with 2 official languages – English and French.

  • Key factors
  • Quality Education
  • Immigration Possibilities
  • Relatively low cost
  • Healthy and Safe Communities
  • High rates of Employment

Germany

Germany is the third most popular destination among international students in the world. More than twelve percent of students at German universities come from abroad – just like you. Germany is an attractive place to study and German university degrees are highly respected by employers worldwide.

  • Key factors
  • Top Quality
  • Free Education
  • Geared to Practice
  • Safe Country
  • Diversity

USA

More international students study in the USA than any other country, and for good reason. While many of the world’s top institutions reside in the US, there are also more than 4,000 other world-class universities offering a top-notch education that can lead to career success. The US remains the source of most of the world’s ground-breaking developments in technology, business, arts, and beyond.

  • Key factors
  • World renowned Academia
  • Engaging Campuses
  • Global Marketability
  • Creative Ideas
  • Cutting Edge Technology

United Kingdom

The UK attracts over 6,00000 international students each year on a variety of programmes, from English language courses to PhDs. The UK education system is flexible, so you can study in a way that suits your lifestyle and career aspirations. When you study in the UK you meet people from different nationalities, sharing their backgrounds and discovering new perspectives.

  • Key factors
  • World's greatest thinkers
  • Multicultural Nation
  • Gateway to Europe
  • Friendly people
  • One-year master

Scandinavia

Scandinavia is Europe’s northernmost region, consisting of Sweden, Denmark, Norway, and Finland. Those four countries are home to some of the best universities in Europe and the world, and you can choose from more than 2,000 Bachelor and Master programmes in total.

  • Key factors
  • No tuition fees
  • international environment
  • Most satisfied Nations
  • Scholarships for Research
  • Unique study style
